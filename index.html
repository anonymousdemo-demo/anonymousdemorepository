Any3DAvatar: Fast and High-Quality Full-Head 3D Avatar Generation from Any Single Portrait Image

Under Review

Abstract

Full 3D head reconstruction from a single image is essential for applications such as virtual/augmented reality, gaming, and telepresence, yet existing methods still face a clear quality-efficiency trade-off: high-fidelity results usually rely on multi-stage pipelines with heavy per-subject optimization, whereas fast feed-forward approaches often lose geometric accuracy and fine appearance details. To bridge this gap, we propose \textbf{Any3DAvatar}, a fast and high-quality method for single-image 3D Gaussian head avatar generation, whose fastest setting reconstructs a full head in under one second while preserving high-fidelity geometry and texture.Our framework is built on three key components. First, we construct a more diverse training dataset to reduce selection bias and improve fine-detail reconstruction under broader identities and conditions, and we further derive a small benchmark split for full-head quality evaluation. Second, we design a feed-forward denoising network that, conditioned on spatially-aware noisy tokens, directly decodes a 3D Gaussian point cloud for one-step reconstruction. Third, we introduce a multi-task learning scheme that jointly optimizes geometry and appearance. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Any3DAvatar delivers consistently superior rendering quality at high speed, significantly outperforming prior 3D full head reconstruction methods

Method

Pipeline of Any3DAvatar. Given a single portrait, we extract image tokens and Gaussian tokens, jointly denoise them with a DiT backbone, and decode the denoised tokens into both multi-view images and 3D Gaussian point clouds; during training, we further apply a multi-task objective combining reconstruction and perceptual supervision, while inference uses a single-step feed-forward generation path.
